01The problem it solves

Expense trackers have a retention problem: logging what you spend is a chore with no feedback loop, so most people quit within a week — and students rarely find out where the allowance went. CoinQuest reframes the habit as a game: the monthly budget is a health bar, consistent logging builds streaks and XP, and a local AI coach turns the numbers into advice — or a roast. Tracking money should feel like maintaining a save file, not filing taxes.

03Constraints — what it will not do
  • No accounts, logins, or multi-user. One player, on localhost.
  • No bank sync, card data, or real financial integrations.
  • No cloud deployment. Data never leaves the machine.
  • The AI never does arithmetic. Python computes every number; the model only writes text.
  • AI output is advisory only. It never creates, edits, or deletes records.
  • One person + one AI assistant, five days. Features that threaten the deadline move down a tier.
Definition of done
  • Clone, run one command, add an expense, watch the HP bar drop.
