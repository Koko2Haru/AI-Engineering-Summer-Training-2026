# Action Verb Reference

Used for action-verb analysis, bullet rewrites, and flagging weak/passive phrasing. Organized by the type of accomplishment being described — pick the verb that matches what actually happened, don't swap in a "stronger" verb for something that didn't occur.

## Leadership & Management

Led, Directed, Managed, Oversaw, Coordinated, Orchestrated, Mentored, Championed, Delegated, Supervised, Chaired, Spearheaded, Facilitated, Guided

## Achievement & Results

Achieved, Delivered, Exceeded, Surpassed, Attained, Won, Secured, Generated, Increased, Reduced, Improved, Boosted, Accelerated, Maximized, Minimized

## Building & Creating

Built, Designed, Architected, Developed, Engineered, Launched, Created, Established, Founded, Implemented, Constructed, Formulated, Initiated, Pioneered, Prototyped

## Technical / Engineering

Automated, Deployed, Migrated, Refactored, Optimized, Debugged, Integrated, Configured, Provisioned, Containerized, Scaled, Instrumented, Benchmarked

## Analysis & Research

Analyzed, Investigated, Evaluated, Diagnosed, Audited, Identified, Modeled, Forecasted, Quantified, Synthesized, Assessed, Validated

## Communication & Collaboration

Presented, Authored, Negotiated, Advised, Consulted, Persuaded, Documented, Trained, Onboarded, Collaborated, Partnered, Liaised

## Process Improvement

Streamlined, Standardized, Overhauled, Restructured, Consolidated, Modernized, Simplified, Eliminated

## Financial / Business Impact

Budgeted, Allocated, Reduced (costs), Grew (revenue), Forecasted, Monetized, Negotiated (contracts), Cut (spend)

## Weak Verbs & Phrases to Flag

These aren't grammatically wrong, but they describe *duties* rather than *outcomes*, and should almost always be replaced or paired with a stronger result-oriented follow-through:

| Weak Phrase | Why It's Weak | Better Direction |
|---|---|---|
| "Responsible for..." | States assigned duty, not what was actually accomplished | Replace with the action itself: "Managed X" not "Responsible for managing X" |
| "Helped with..." / "Assisted with..." | Downplays actual contribution and ownership | Name the specific action taken, even within a team effort: "Contributed database schema design for..." |
| "Worked on..." | Vague, no outcome implied | Replace with what was actually built/changed/achieved |
| "Involved in..." | Passive, unclear ownership | State the specific role/action |
| "Duties included..." | Reads like a job description, not an achievement | Rewrite as first-person (implied) achievement bullets |
| "Tasked with..." | Focuses on assignment, not delivery | Focus on what was delivered as a result |
| "Participated in..." | Ambiguous level of contribution | Specify the actual contribution |

## Weak Verbs (Overused / Low-Signal)

Did, Made, Used, Handled, Dealt with, Was in charge of, Got, Gave, Took care of

These aren't forbidden but are usually a sign a more precise, stronger verb is available. "Handled customer complaints" → "Resolved an average of 30 customer escalations weekly, maintaining a 95% satisfaction rating."

## Detecting Repetitive Verb Usage

If the same strong verb ("Led," "Managed," "Built") appears more than 2-3 times across the resume, flag it as repetitive even though each individual instance is a "strong" verb — variety matters for readability and signals a broader range of contribution types. Suggest synonyms from the same category above rather than defaulting back to a weak verb.
